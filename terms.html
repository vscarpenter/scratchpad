Plain-language terms for a local-only app.

Scratchpad is a free, local-only notes app. There is no account, no server, and no relationship to manage. These terms exist mainly to set expectations and to make clear what the app does and does not promise.

Using Scratchpad

By loading Scratchpad in your browser, you agree to these terms. If you don't agree, the remedy is simple: close the tab. No account exists to deactivate, and no data is held anywhere outside your browser.

What Scratchpad is

  • A static web page that runs entirely in your browser.
  • Notes are stored in your browser's IndexedDB. Nothing is uploaded.
  • There is no backend, no account system, no telemetry, and no advertising.
  • The full privacy policy describes the data posture in detail.

Your data is your responsibility

Because everything lives in this browser, the only copy of your notes is the one on this device. Use About → Export all notes (JSON) to back them up. Clearing site data, switching browsers, switching devices, or using private browsing will erase the notes that haven't been exported.

Scratchpad cannot recover notes that have been deleted from your browser. There is no server-side copy to restore from.

Acceptable use
